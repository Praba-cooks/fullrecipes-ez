Buttermilk Formula

Refreshing Pudina Buttermilk

A traditional sour-milk emulsion accented with crushed volatile herbal extracts for instantaneous physical cooling.

Prep Time: 10 Mins
Temperature: Chilled
Yield: 2 Servings

Required Ingredients

Preparation Method

  1. Mince the mint leaves and green chili into a fine consistency to break open oil pockets.
  2. Whisk the fresh curd until completely smooth and free of large fat solids.
  3. Pour in the chilled water, add the processed herbs, cumin powder, and salt, then blend briefly until slightly frothy.
Dessert Formula

Tamil Nadu Style Rava Laddu

A signature festive confection made from roasted semolina crystals, sugar, and rich clarified butter, hand-shaped into dense sweet spheres.

Prep Time: 25 Mins
Temperature: Medium Heat
Yield: 10-12 Laddus

Required Ingredients

Preparation Method

  1. Dry roast the semolina in a thick-bottomed pan on low flame until highly fragrant. Do not color change or brown the rava. Set aside to cool.
  2. Heat ghee in another pan and fry the cashew fractions and raisins until light golden brown.
  3. Pulse the cooled rava slightly with sugar in a grinder. Mix with cardamom, fried nuts, and warm ghee. Add milk in small increments, compress firmly, and shape into uniform spheres.
Variety Rice Selection

Thakkali Sadam (Tomato Rice)

A highly popular Tamil Nadu style savory rice prepared by folding cooked rice into a spiced, concentrated tomato and onion reduction sauce.

Prep Time: 30 Mins
Flavor Profile: Tangy & Savory
Grain State: Separated Grains

Required Ingredients

Preparation Method

  1. Splutter mustard seeds in hot sesame oil, then fry chana dal until golden.
  2. Sauté onions followed by chopped tomatoes until completely soft and oil begins to separate around the edges (Thokku consistency).
  3. Add the cooled pre-cooked rice to the pan and gently mix to uniform distribution without breaking the structural shape of the grains.
Variety Rice Selection

Thengai Sadam (Coconut Rice)

A mild and highly aromatic preparation combining fresh, delicately flaked coconut flesh with tempered spices and non-sticky rice grains.

Prep Time: 20 Mins
Flavor Profile: Mild & Nutty
Grain State: Separated Grains

Required Ingredients

Preparation Method

  1. Heat coconut oil in a pan, temper mustard seeds, urad dal, cashews, ginger, and curry leaves until fragrant.
  2. Turn heat parameters down to low, add the grated coconut, and toss for 1-2 minutes just to dry surface moisture without color change.
  3. Introduce your pre-cooked rice, add salt to taste, and blend carefully with a soft spatula until warm.
Variety Rice Selection

Puli Sadam (Tamarind Rice)

A traditional South Indian travel staple showcasing complex tangy depths, produced via a robust tamarind reduction paste and signature dry roasted spice powder.

Prep Time: 40 Mins
Flavor Profile: Bold & Tangy
Grain State: Separated Grains

Required Ingredients

Preparation Method

  1. Dry roast fenugreek, coriander seeds, and red chilis in a hot skillet, grind to a fine powder.
  2. Heat gingelly oil in a pan, fry tempering ingredients including peanuts, then add the tamarind water extract. Simmer until oil surfaces on top. Stir in the ground powder.
  3. Combine sections of this thick tamarind sauce directly into cooled rice, turning carefully until every grain is dark golden.
Variety Rice Selection

Elumichai Sadam (Lemon Rice)

A bright, refreshing lunchtime classic that pairs aromatic curcumin-infused oil with raw, unheated citrus juice to retain crisp flavor integrity.

Prep Time: 20 Mins
Flavor Profile: Zesty & Vibrant
Grain State: Separated Grains

Required Ingredients

Preparation Method

  1. Heat oil, sizzle mustard seeds, and toast the peanuts, urad dal, chilis, and curry leaves until golden brown.
  2. Stir in turmeric powder to color the oil, then remove the skillet entirely from the heat grid. Let it cool for 60 seconds before pouring in fresh lemon juice.
  3. Gently fold the cooled citrus mixture through the white rice grains until uniformly yellow.
Biryani Varieties

Dindigul Seeraga Samba Mutton Biryani

An authentic regional specialty using short, aromatic Seeraga Samba grains to maximize absorption of mutton fat, curd, and peppery spice notes.

Prep Time: 50 Mins
Flavor Profile: Spicy & Peppery
Grain Base: Seeraga Samba Rice

Required Ingredients

Preparation Method

  1. Sauté crushed shallots, ginger-garlic paste, and green chilis in hot ghee until transparent.
  2. Incorporate mutton pieces and yogurt; pressure cook the meat base until tender and succulent.
  3. Add water matching your rice measurement (1:2 ratio), bring to a full boil, fold in the soaked rice, cover, and steam-cook on low heat for 15 minutes.
Biryani Varieties

Fine Kolkata Style Chicken Biryani

A refined, royal preparation layered with par-boiled Basmati rice, soft spiced potatoes, and a delicate splash of rose, keora, and saffron-infused milk extracts.

Prep Time: 60 Mins
Flavor Profile: Subtle & Fragrant
Grain Base: Long-Grain Basmati

Required Ingredients

Preparation Method

  1. Boil peeled potato halves in a spiced chicken broth until tender and infused with flavor. Cook the chicken separately in a rich gravy.
  2. Par-boil long-grain Basmati rice in hot water containing whole spices until exactly 70% cooked, then drain.
  3. Layer chicken gravy, spiced potatoes, and par-boiled rice in a heavy pot. Drizzle saffron milk, atar, and fried onions over the top. Seal and cook on low heat (Dum) for 20 minutes.
Biryani Varieties

Ambur Style Chicken Biryani

An iconic Arcot-style biryani prepared by cooking meat pieces in a smooth, vibrant red chili paste base, then combined with boiled Seeraga Samba rice.

Prep Time: 45 Mins
Flavor Profile: Savory & Balanced
Grain Base: Seeraga Samba Rice

Required Ingredients

Preparation Method

  1. Sauté onions, ginger-garlic paste, and tomatoes. Stir in the red chili paste and yogurt, then cook chicken pieces inside this base to form a gravy (Yakhni).
  2. In a separate large pot, boil water and par-cook the Seeraga Samba grains until firm but pliable. Drain thoroughly.
  3. Incorporate the hot rice directly into the bubbling chicken gravy. Close tightly with a lid and steam on low charcoal heat indicators for 15 minutes.
Biryani Varieties

Hyderabadi Dum Mutton Biryani

The classic Kacchi method where raw, enzyme-tenderized mutton is layered directly under partially boiled Basmati rice, then slow-steamed to perfection.

Prep Time: 75 Mins
Flavor Profile: Rich, Robust & Spicy
Grain Base: Extra-Long Basmati

Required Ingredients

Preparation Method

  1. Marinate raw mutton with green papaya paste, yogurt, spices, and fried onions for 4 hours to break down protein bonds.
  2. Boil Basmati rice and layer it over the raw meat base in stages: first layer at 50% cooked, middle layer at 70%, and top layer at 90%.
  3. Seal the lid onto the heavy pot using wheat dough to create a airtight seal. Cook on high heat for 15 minutes, then reduce to a low setting for 45 minutes.
Biryani Varieties

Thalassery Chicken Biryani

A legendary Malabar coast recipe using small, thin Khaima grains cooked entirely in ghee and paired with a fiery, green-chili-dominant chicken masala.

Prep Time: 55 Mins
Flavor Profile: Warm & Highly Aromatic
Grain Base: Khaima / Jeerakasala Rice

Required Ingredients

Preparation Method

  1. Wash and dry the short Khaima grains, then pan-toast them in warm ghee before boiling to keep them perfectly distinct.
  2. Sauté onions, ginger, garlic, and green chili paste. Toss in the chicken and spice blend, cooking down until it forms a thick, clinging masala layer.
  3. Layer the ghee-cooked rice over the thick chicken masala base, top with fried nuts and raisins, seal tightly, and steam-cook on low heat for 15 minutes.
